NISS (Normal/Inverse Scramble Switching) is a core FMC technique that allows a solver to
explore continuations on the inverse scramble and then combine both the normal and inverse
parts into a single solution. 


### Rules
- cube state on normal contain inverse of moves made on inverse, scramble on normal, and moves on normal
- cube state on inverse contain inverse of normal moves, inverse of scramble and moves on inverse
- user is able to niss between steps or within steps
- on multiline solution view inverse move are presented in bracets
- saved steps solution list should also contain moves on inverse in brackets
